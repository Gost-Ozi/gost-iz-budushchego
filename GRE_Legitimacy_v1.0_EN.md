# GRE Legitimacy & Coordination Layer v1.0

**Status:** baseline version, ready for publication  
**Date:** April 2026  
**Based on:** GRE Core v1.0, Protocol v1.0, Economics v1.0, Admission & Competence v1.0, Foundational Layers v1.0  
**Purpose:** define the conditions for the legitimacy of collective decisions (authority) and mechanisms to terminate illegitimate authority.

---

## 1. Definition of Authority in GRE

**Authority** is a temporary function of coordinating the actions of a multitude of R to preserve and increase Φ, delegated voluntarily and limited by the invariants of GRE.

Authority is **not** a source of privilege, an inherited or lifelong status, or a basis for violating the D, P, or F of any R.

---

## 2. Scope of Legitimacy

### 2.1. For those who have accepted GRE
Full jurisdiction: invariants, protocol, economics, admission procedures.

### 2.2. For those who have not accepted GRE (guests, neighbors, temporary residents)
- The basic invariants (I1–I4) are binding on everyone within the territory of the GRE community.
- Other rules of GRE do not apply, but they must not disrupt the public order of the community or interfere with its life.

---

## 3. Legitimacy of Decisions in the Absence of an Explicit Vote

### 3.1. Principle of "no voice — no vote"
Silence is not consent. If a subject has not explicitly expressed a position, their vote is considered **null** — it does not affect the result and cannot be used for manipulation.

### 3.2. The decisive vote of one
If at least one subject participates in the vote (provided the procedure is open and everyone had an opportunity to speak), their vote is **decisive** for that decision.

### 3.3. Recording null votes
The fixator must record in the registry: "the following R were notified, no objections were received, their votes are null."

---

## 4. Separation of Roles and Small Communities

### 4.1. Principle of separation
The functions of fixator (registry), arbitrator (dispute resolution), economic operator (Ba accrual), and Core modifier cannot be combined in the same person. The prohibition applies **legally** and **technically** (via registry code).

### 4.2. Exception for small communities (up to 10 R)
The community may **refuse** these functions and act according to the direct protocol from Core. All significant actions are recorded by an **external fixator** (from another community, a district fixator, or an AI via communication link).

### 4.3. Restoring separation
When the number of members exceeds 10 R, the community must implement role separation within 3 months.

---

## 5. Legitimacy Between Communities

### 5.1. Principle of consensus seeking
In a conflict between GRE communities, they must:
1. Hold a joint meeting of representatives.
2. Seek a **solution acceptable to all** (consensus).
3. If consensus is impossible, invite an **external arbitrator**.
4. If arbitration is impossible, **freeze the conflict** by recording the status quo.

### 5.2. Global arbitrator
For disputes involving more than 3 communities or global Φ, a **Council of Fixators** may be established (elected by communities for 2 years). Council decisions require ratification by the parties to the conflict.

---

## 6. Termination of Legitimacy and the Right to Resist

### 6.1. Automatic loss of legitimacy
Authority loses legitimacy **immediately**, without additional decisions, if it:

1. Commits **a non-isolated intentional action that worsens the lives of the majority, is unjustifiable, and is raised by at least one percent of vassals**.  
   *(That is: violations are not singular, are committed intentionally, lead to a real deterioration in the quality of life of most R, cannot be justified by extreme necessity, and the fact of the violation has been publicly recorded by an initiative group comprising at least 1% of the total number of R in the community.)*

2. Refuses to review its decisions through the GRE Protocol (Level 2).

3. Uses authority for mass personal enrichment (proven by the registry).

4. Commits an act of annexation, occupation, or genocide (even outside GRE territory).

**In these cases, the authority ceases to be GRE authority. Subjects have the right not to recognize it and to resist.**

### 6.2. Right to resist
Any R or group of R has the right to **active non-violent resistance**: refusal to obey illegitimate orders, public recording of violations in the registry, coordination with other GRE communities to isolate the authority.

**If non-violent methods are exhausted and the authority continues to massively violate P** (threat to life, liberty), subjects have the right to take **minimally necessary violent action** to stop those violations. Such action falls under Core clause 4.1 (situational action) and requires post-verification.

### 6.3. Post-overthrow
After a change of authority:
1. The new authority must undergo **legitimization procedures** (open vote, recognition by communities).
2. All acts of violence by the resistance are subject to **verification** (whether they were minimally necessary). If violated, the guilty parties are held accountable.
3. Debts of the illegitimate authority are **not transferred** to the new authority unless otherwise agreed by consensus.

### 6.4. Limits
GRE does **not** permit: violence as a first resort, mass purges based on "guilty because served," annexation and occupation as a way to "impose GRE order." Any resistance, even justifiable, must strive for **minimal violence** and **maximum compensation** (in Ba) to victims.

---

## 7. Constraints on Authority (Safeguards)

- **Reversibility of decisions** — any decision can be reviewed through the Protocol (Level 2).
- **Term limits** — maximum of 3 years, then reconfirmation.
- **Automatic loss of legitimacy** — according to clause 6.1.
- **Recall procedure** — initiated by any R, recorded by a fixator, decided by vote (⅔) or arbitration.

---

## 8. Past Debts and Responsibility

### 8.1. Principle of "others' sins do not worsen the lives of the innocent"
Debts (environmental, social, economic) accumulated before the adoption of GRE are **not automatically transferred** to new subjects.

### 8.2. Who is responsible
Specific R (or their heirs) who benefited from these debts and had the opportunity to prevent the harm but did not do so are responsible for past debts. Once identified, such R must compensate for the damage in Ba (by arbitration decision or consensus).

### 8.3. Protection of the innocent
If the perpetrators cannot be identified or no longer exist, the community is **not obliged** to pay their debts. Instead, it may: recognize the debt as "historical injustice" (record it in the registry), restore Φ through voluntary projects (without coercion).

**Real money does not disappear.** If someone is found to have enriched themselves from a crisis, GRE requires them to: compensate the victims, answer to the community (restriction of rights, community service).

---

## 9. Minimum Quorum for Decisions

For decisions affecting the lives of **more than 10 R**, a minimum quorum of **20%** of the total number of R entitled to vote in that community is required. If the quorum is not met, the decision is **not considered legitimate** and is not recorded in the registry as binding. An exception is made for emergency situations (section 10), where the quorum may be temporarily reduced to **10%** with mandatory post-ratification.

---

## 10. Emergency Establishment of Authority (Post-Collapse)

If the previous authority has collapsed and no new one has been created, GRE allows the **emergency establishment of temporary authority**: an initiative group of **at least three R** from three different local GRE communities may form a **temporary coordination council**. Such a council is valid for no more than **30 days** (maximum +30 by decision of the fixators). During this term, it must hold an open vote to legitimize permanent authority or dissolve.

---

## 11. Relationship with Other Layers

- **Core** → invariants as basic constraints.
- **Protocol** → recall, review, recording.
- **Economics** → compensation, limits on enrichment.
- **Admission** → requirements for authority holders.
- **Foundational** → axioms of truth, responsibility, development.
- **Registry** → recording of all decisions and precedents.
